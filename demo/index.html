CycleBench

Hormone labs are rare. Wearables are daily. We turn everyday signals — heart rate, glucose, sleep, symptoms — into a day-by-day estimate of where someone is in their menstrual cycle, so researchers can build and compare AI fairly.

Research infrastructure only — not a diagnosis or medical advice. Everything on this page is a SynthCycle participant (fake, open, no patient data).

1 · Fit (train only)
Learn from mcPHASES train

25 real train people. Per cycle phase: mean + std of each wearable/CGM/sleep/symptom, plus a covariance matrix (signals that move together).

2 · Sample
Draw 120 new people

Each day: multivariate normal draw from that phase’s mean + covariance. Clip to physiological ranges. Smooth slightly day-to-day.

3 · Label
Phase from calendar rule

Day-in-cycle → menstrual / follicular / ovulatory / luteal. Labels are not from the Gradient Boosting model.

4 · Mess
~8% holes on purpose

We delete values at random so the open cohort looks as gappy as real life. Gaps stay empty in the demo — not imputed for display.

What CycleBench includes

The reusable research layer left behind — not a wellness wrapper.

  • Canonical daily-timeline data schema with observation fields; missingness preserved as NaN (never silently filled in the release)
  • Source adapter and provenance-manifest workflow (mcPHASES local + SynthCycle open)
  • Participant-level train / validation / test splits with leakage checks; trailing 7-day windows for time-aware features
  • Reproducible Gradient Boosting baseline and evaluator (seeded, one-command rerun)
  • Metrics: macro-F1, balanced accuracy, calibration (ECE), and modality ablations
  • Demo timeline distinguishing observed values, model-inferred phases (confidence-aware), and missing data

Pipeline in one line: learn phase-wise stats from real train only → sample open SynthCycle (calendar phase labels) → train GBM on synthetic → score on real held-out people (TSTR). We do not train on real to label synthetic.
